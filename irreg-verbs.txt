1. The Athematic & Suppletive "Big Eight"

These are the foundational irregulars. They often combine ancient athematic present systems with suppletive perfect systems.
Verb	Principal Parts	Key Irregularities
sum	sum, esse, fui, futurus	Highly suppletive; root es- vs fu-; athematic present.
possum	possum, posse, potui	Compound of pote + sum; infinitive is posse (not possere).
volo	volo, velle, volui	Irregular present (vis, vult); unique infinitive and subjunctive (velim).
nolo	nolo, nolle, nolui	Contraction of ne + volo; imperative noli is vital for prohibitions.
malo	malo, malle, malui	Contraction of magis + volo.
fero	fero, ferre, tuli, latus	Massive suppletion (fer-, tul-, lat-); athematic present (fers, fert).
eo	eo, ire, ii (ivi), itum	Root i- changes to e- before vowels (eo, eunt); irregular future (ibo).
fio	fio, fieri, factus sum	Serves as the passive of facio; active forms with passive meaning.
2. The "Eating" Verb (Edo)

While it can be conjugated regularly as a 3rd conjugation verb, edo frequently uses athematic forms that are identical to forms of sum.

    edo, edere (esse), edi, esum (to eat)

        es (you eat) vs. es (you are)

        est (he eats) vs. est (he is)

        esse (to eat) vs. esse (to be)

3. Semi-Deponent Verbs

These verbs are regular in the present system but switch to passive forms (with active meanings) in the perfect system.

    audeo, audere, ausus sum: to dare

    gaudeo, gaudere, gavisus sum: to rejoice

    soleo, solere, solitus sum: to be accustomed to

    fido, fidere, fisus sum: to trust (and its compounds: confido, diffido)

4. Defective Verbs (Non-Impersonal)

These verbs lack a full suite of forms. The "Preteritive" verbs below have perfect forms but present meanings.
Preteritive Verbs

    memini, meminisse: to remember (Perfect forms translated as present).

    odi, odisse: to hate (Perfect forms translated as present).

    coepi, coepisse: to have begun (Only exists in perfect system).

Others

    aio: I say/assert (Mostly used in ait or aiunt).

    inquam: I say (Used almost exclusively in direct quotes).

    fari: to speak (Mostly poetic; lacks many present indicative forms).

    quaeo / nequeo: I am able / I am unable (Conjugated like eo, but many forms are rare).

    ave / salve: (Imperative only) greetings.

5. Significant Compounds

While these follow the patterns of their roots, their prefix changes often mask their identity.

    prosum, prodesse, profui: (to be useful) Inserts a -d- when the root begins with a vowel (pro-d-est).

    absum / adsum: (sum compounds).

    redeo, abeo, exeo, pereo: (eo compounds). Note that ambio (to go around) is actually a regular 4th conjugation verb.

    affero, confero, differo, effero, infero, offero, refero: (fero compounds) These exhibit various phonetic assimilations (e.g., ad + fero = affero).

6. The Oddity of Do

    do, dare, dedi, datus: Unlike other 1st conjugation verbs, the a is short (ă) in most forms (e.g., dămus, dătis, dăre), except for das and the 2nd person singular imperative da.
